### S96-F02 — R8 cross-reference caps and direct empirical measurement
**Date:** 2026-04-22
**Severity:** Medium
**Context:** Ja Morant Skill 2 scoring surfaced that R8 cross-reference caps, applied mechanically, would pull down three sub-domains (#5 off-dribble, #6 mid-range, #20 def-rebounding) despite direct 2024-25 statistical measurement validating scores above the cap. Example: #6 Mid-range FG% 47.8% on 67 FGA anchors an 8.0 per the rubric, but cross-ref cap from #24 (Shot selection = 6.0) would mechanically force #6 to 6.0. Tyler approved not applying the cap in this case.
**Learning:** When a sub-domain score is anchored by direct empirical measurement (full stat pipeline present, not extrapolation), and that measurement validates a score above the R8 cross-reference cap, the cap does NOT bind. Cross-reference caps are intended to constrain extrapolated or projected scores — they are not meant to override observed on-court reality. Applies to NBA veteran evaluations with complete 2024-25 stat pulls; applies differently to prospect cases where scores are inferred from qualitative signals. Document the call in the Cross-Reference Log: "Cap considered, not applied — direct measurement validates above cap."
**Applies to:** Skill 2 scoring step — R8 cross-reference application. Affects any NBA-vet evaluation where mechanical R8 caps would bind against direct statistical support.
