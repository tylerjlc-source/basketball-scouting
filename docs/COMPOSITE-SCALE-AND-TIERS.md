# COMPOSITE SCALE AND TIERS — Scout-composite Reference

---

## ARCHITECTURAL STATEMENT

The composite is assigned, not calculated.

The tier table below is the primary assignment tool. The evaluator reads the completed 26 sub-domain profile, identifies which tier band the player fits, and assigns the composite from within that band. The anchor examples in each band are the calibration mechanism — they define what a profile in that range looks like in practice.

Domain scores exist as a readable profile summary only. They are not calculation inputs. Nothing in the composite assignment depends on domain scores mathematically.

Archetype narrows which anchor examples are the most relevant comparison set. It does not change the assignment process.

Load this document for every evaluation. Do not assign a composite without it.

---

## COMPOSITE SCALE DEFINITION

Composite scores are expressed to two decimal places (e.g. 8.47, 9.23).
Range: 1.00–10.00.

**Rationale:** 26 sub-domain inputs scored to one decimal place each produce genuine separation between players. Two decimal places is earned precision — it reflects the specificity of the inputs. It is not false precision.

## TIER ASSIGNMENT PROTOCOL

This protocol converts a completed 26 sub-domain profile into a composite score. It replaces formula-based aggregation. The evaluator follows these steps in order.

---

### Step 1 — Profile inventory

Read all 26 sub-domain scores and classify each:

- **Strengths** — sub-domains scoring ≥7.0
- **Liabilities** — sub-domains scoring ≤4.9
- **Structural zeros** — sub-domains expected to score low by archetype design (e.g. rim protection for a Guard, post offense for a Shooting Specialist). These are not liabilities. They do not count against the player regardless of tier.

Record the count of each category before proceeding.

---

### Step 2 — Survivability check

- Did the player pass all non-negotiables for their position? A failed gate caps the composite ceiling regardless of strengths. Apply the cap before assigning a band.
- Count non-structural liabilities only (structural zeros excluded). More than 3 genuine liabilities is a floor signal — pulls the candidate band down before anchor comparison.

---

### Step 3 — Candidate band identification

Map the strength/liability balance to a starting band:

| Profile shape | Candidate band |
|---|---|
| Elite across nearly all 26 sub-domains. Zero liabilities — structural zeros do not count. | Tiers 1–3 (9.20–10.00) |
| Elite at primary sub-domains for archetype. Minimal drag — 1 liability at most. | Tiers 4–5 (8.60–9.19) |
| Above average across position-relevant sub-domains. No structural liabilities. | Tiers 6–7 (7.90–8.59) |
| Solid across most sub-domains. Some gaps — 2–3 liabilities acceptable. | Tier 8 (7.50–7.89) |
| Clear strengths in primary sub-domains. Noticeable gaps elsewhere. | Tier 9 (6.80–7.49) |
| One or two real strengths. Significant gaps across most sub-domains. | Tier 10 (5.80–6.79) |
| No transferable strengths at NBA level. | Tiers 11–13 (1.00–5.79) |

This is the starting point only. Anchor comparison in Step 4 confirms or adjusts it.

---

### Step 4 — Anchor comparison and awards cross-check

Select 2–3 anchors from the candidate band that match the player's archetype and profile shape. Compare directly:

- Is this player better, worse, or similar to those anchors at the sub-domains that matter most for the archetype?
- If the player compares favorably to most anchors in the band → confirm the band or consider moving one band up.
- If the player compares unfavorably to most anchors in the band → move one band down.
- If the candidate band has no populated anchors yet → hold the band assignment from Step 3 and flag for review as the library grows.

Anchor comparison is the primary calibration mechanism. The library becomes more precise as more players are confirmed.

**Anchor-state correction — apply before the comparison.**

When a candidate-band anchor carries an active deflation flag in the ANCHOR-LIBRARY Notes column (active recovery, injury flag, dormancy, load management), its current composite is suppressed below its healthy-state ceiling. Use the healthy-state ceiling — not the deflated current composite — as the comparison reference, and document the correction in the walkthrough (e.g., "Tatum 8.88 currently / ~9.05 healthy per S108-F01"). Especially relevant when the candidate band has high deflation density (e.g., Tier 4–5 wings with Tatum, Kawhi, and others flagged simultaneously).

Distinct from anchor-set scope errors (S102-F03): this addresses anchor *state* (a flagged anchor is itself deflated), not anchor *selection* (evaluator picked too narrow a comparison set). Run both checks at this step.

---

**Awards cross-check — run after anchor comparison, before finalizing band placement.**

Career award credentials are independent consensus signals from the people who watch every game. If credentials suggest a higher placement than the sub-domain profile is producing, recheck the drag sub-domains before locking — the credential is a recheck trigger, not an override. Recency matters — awards outside the evaluation window do not anchor current rating. Team awards (championships, Finals) reflect team quality as much as individual and are contextual only.

| Award | Tier signal |
|---|---|
| MVP | Tier 4 floor minimum. Multiple MVPs push toward Tier 3. |
| Finals MVP | Tier 4 floor. Context matters — strength of opponent and team context apply. |
| All-NBA First Team | Tier 5 floor. One of the five best players in the league that season. |
| All-NBA Second Team | Tier 5 floor. Borderline — could sit at top of Tier 6. |
| All-NBA Third Team | Tier 6 floor. One of the 15 best players in the league that season. |
| All-Defensive First Team | Tier 6 floor. Cross-check defensive sub-domains (#15, #16, #17, #18). |
| DPOY | Tier 6 floor. Cross-check #15, #16, #17, #18 — if sub-domains don't support it, recheck before locking. |
| All-Star starter | Upper Tier 7 minimum. Voted by fans as one of the two best at their position — stronger signal than reserve. |
| All-Star selection (reserve) | Tier 7 floor at time of selection. Multiple consecutive selections suggest sustained Tier 7+. |
| All-Defensive Second Team | Tier 7 floor. Cross-check defensive sub-domains. |
| 6th Man of the Year | Tier 7–8 signal. Best non-starter in the league. Does not anchor a floor the same way All-Star does — role context applies. |
| Rookie of the Year | Directional only. Confirms Tier 7–9 entry level. Does not anchor a floor — best rookie varies by class quality. |
| Most Improved Player | Directional only. Confirms upward trajectory. Does not anchor a floor — starting and ending points vary too widely. |
| NBA Champion | Contextual only. Cross-reference role and individual production. |
| Finals appearance | Contextual only. Directional signal — reflects team quality as much as individual quality. |

**Multiple awards compound.** A player with MVP + Finals MVP + multiple All-NBA First Teams clearly sits in Tier 3–4. The compound signal is stronger than any single award.

---

### Step 5 — Position within the band

Once the band is confirmed, place the player within it using anchor proximity:

| Position | Criteria |
|---|---|
| **Upper third** | Profile is clearly stronger than most anchors in the band. Approaches the floor of the band above. |
| **Middle** | Profile is roughly equivalent to the central anchors in the band. |
| **Lower third** | Profile clears the band floor but does not compare favorably to most anchors within it. |

Within the sub-range identified, **strength cluster density** determines the final decimal placement — how many genuine strengths does the player have relative to the most comparable anchor? More strengths at the archetype-relevant sub-domains pushes the score toward the top of the sub-range. Fewer pushes it toward the floor.

Express the final composite to two decimal places.

---

### Step 5.5 — Prospect Gate (applies to any non-NBA player)

Before finalizing the composite for any prospect, ask: does this composite reflect demonstrated current basketball ability, or is it inflated by ceiling projection?

Apply band evidence requirements:

| Band | Requirement |
|---|---|
| 7.50–7.89 | Default range for highly touted prospects at peak. No additional justification required. |
| 7.90–8.29 | Not appropriate for any prospect who has not yet played NBA regular-season competition. This band requires confirmed NBA performance. |
| 8.30+ | Not appropriate for a prospect without ironclad multi-season evidence at elite competition level. Apply Step 6 ambiguity rule — assign lower band. |

If the composite exceeds 7.89 for a prospect who has not yet played NBA regular-season competition, pull the composite to 7.89 or below. No justification path exists for pre-NBA players above this ceiling. The NBA player carve-out at the bottom of this step applies to rookies rated at current NBA performance level.

This step does not apply to NBA players rated at current level.

---

### Step 6 — Ambiguity resolution

If the profile sits between two adjacent bands after Steps 4 and 5: assign the lower band and flag for review. Do not assign to the upper band without a clear anchor match that supports it.
