# NON-NEGOTIABLES — Layer 1 Scouting Rubric Reference
**Load for every evaluation. Required for position gate checks.**
**Last updated: Session 64 — extracted from POSITION-WEIGHTS-AND-NONNEG.md**

---

## PURPOSE

Non-negotiables are gateway traits. A score below the threshold caps the projection ceiling regardless of how elite the player is everywhere else. The cap is a ceiling on the composite — not a penalty applied to the score itself.

The gate runs after sub-domain scoring is complete, before composite assignment. It is a post-scoring check, independent of archetype.

---

## PROJECTION MODIFIER OVERRIDE — NON-NEGOTIABLE

**Before applying any position cap, evaluate the projection modifier.**

A non-negotiable threshold represents a permanent structural disqualification, not a current developmental gap. The gate closes only when there is no credible development pathway above the threshold.

**Rule:** If the player has a plausible development pathway above the threshold — based on age, skill trajectory, and character/work ethic — the cap is suspended. A position note is added to the output instead:
*"Position risk flagged: [sub-domain] below threshold at current evaluation. Cap suspended — development pathway present. Reassess at next cycle."*

If the pathway is absent or implausible, the cap applies.

**Age guidance:**
- 17–19 year olds: caps suspended on cognitive/skill non-negotiables (decision-making, court vision, shot selection) unless character/work ethic modifier is explicitly negative. Physical non-negotiables (strength, lateral quickness) may still cap if structural ceiling is documented.
- 20–22 year olds: case-by-case. Strong trajectory + high character suspends. Flat trajectory or senior college players near ceiling applies cap.
- 23+ year olds: cap applies. Development runway is largely exhausted.

**Position shift note:** If a cap is suspended due to trajectory, also evaluate whether a position shift better fits the current profile. A 17-year-old PG with poor decision-making but elite shooting, finishing, and defense may be better evaluated as an SG. Flag this in the output — do not force the position if a better fit exists.

**All position non-negotiables below are subject to this override.**

## NON-NEGOTIABLES BY POSITION

---

### Point Guard

| Sub-domain | Threshold | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| #12 Decision-making | 5/10 | Below average decision-making disqualifies the PG role. They are a scorer playing out of position. Every other PG skill becomes irrelevant if they cannot execute the right play under pressure. |
| #10 Ball security | 4/10 | Chronic turnovers make a PG a net negative possession creator. Protecting the ball is not optional at this position. |
| #11 Court vision | 4/10 | A PG who cannot read the floor cannot run an offense regardless of athleticism, handle, or scoring ability. |

**The position logic:** The PG non-negotiables are all cognitive. You can be slow and still be Tony Parker. You cannot be a decision-making liability and still run an offense.

---

### Shooting Guard

| Sub-domain | Threshold | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| #4 Catch-and-shoot 3PT | 4/10 | Primary offensive floor. If #4 clears, #5 (off-dribble) is irrelevant to survivability — the 3-and-D archetype is a fully viable NBA role. |
| #14 Off-ball movement | 4/10 | Secondary gate — only activates if #4 fails. A SG below both #4 and #14 has no perimeter offensive mechanism and cannot justify their roster spot on offense. |
| #22 Lateral quickness | 4/10 | Below this, teams hunt the SG on every defensive possession. Offensive production cannot compensate for a player who cannot survive defensively. |

*Note: #5 (off-dribble shooting) is NOT a non-negotiable. A SG who catches and shoots and can guard their assignment is a viable NBA player. Off-dribble creation is an archetype differentiator, not a survival threshold.*

---

### Small Forward

| Sub-domain | Threshold | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| #15 On-ball pressure | 4/10 | An SF who cannot guard their primary assignment is a liability on half of all possessions. No offensive output compensates for a wing who cannot be left on a ball handler. |
| #16 Help defense | 4/10 | The SF must rotate, switch, and function in team defensive schemes. Below 4 means they cannot cover any secondary assignment — they are a structural hole in every defensive scheme. |
| #22 Lateral quickness | 4/10 | The physical prerequisite for multi-position defense. Without it, the SF cannot guard guards on switches or recover on closeouts. Defensive versatility — the SF's defining trait — collapses. |

**The position logic:** All three SF non-negotiables are defensive. Versatility is the SF's non-negotiable, and at this position versatility is primarily a defensive property. An SF who cannot defend is a small PF or a large SG — a player whose positioning creates a structural problem.

---

### Power Forward

| Sub-domain | Threshold | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| #20 Defensive rebounding | 4/10 | A PF who does not rebound is a wing playing too close to the basket. Below this threshold they are contributing to the opponent's second-chance economy on every possession. |
| #23 Strength | 4/10 | Cannot hold post position, box out, or be displaced consistently on interior contests. A PF without functional strength is physically miscast — no technique overcomes this structural floor. |
| #16 Help defense | 4/10 | The modern PF is the defensive hub of most NBA schemes — rotating to the dunker spot, switching onto guards, protecting the paint. Below 4 means they cannot function in any scheme-dependent defense. |

*Note: strength (#23) is a physical non-negotiable — developmental pathway is more limited than for cognitive sub-domains. A 17-year-old at 185 lbs with a projectable frame still suspends the cap. A 22-year-old at 185 lbs with a filled-out frame does not.*

**The position logic:** PF non-negotiables are physical and defensive. A PF who rebounds, defends, and does nothing else has a viable NBA role. A PF who cannot do those things does not.

---

### Center

| Sub-domain | Threshold | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| #17 Rim protection | 5/10 | Every team in the NBA attacks the basket. A C who cannot protect the rim removes the primary justification for playing a traditional center. Threshold set at 5 rather than 4 because below-average rim protection at C forces the entire defense to compensate on every single possession. |
| #20 Defensive rebounding | 5/10 | A C who does not rebound is surplus to requirements. Below 5 means they are being consistently outrebounded from the position that should prevent second chances. Threshold at 5 for the same structural reason as rim protection. |
| #23 Strength | 4/10 | Cannot hold post position, gets backed down, loses box-out battles, cannot set screens that create displacement. The entire interior game collapses without it. |

*Same physical non-negotiable note applies as PF — strength ceiling is more age-dependent than skill sub-domains.*

**The position logic:** The C thresholds on rim protection and rebounding are set at 5 rather than 4 because the cost of failure is structurally higher. A rim protection failure by a C results in a layup or dunk. A rebounding failure results in a second-chance possession. The C has the least defensive redundancy — when they fail, the failure is visible on every possession.
