## Section 3 — Scouting Narrative

Amen Thompson at 23 is a profile that doesn't fit any traditional wing archetype cleanly — elite athlete-defender, non-shooter, role-limited offensive contributor operating at star-role minutes (37.4 MPG in 2025-26) on a winning Houston team. The 2024-25 All-Defensive First Team selection (74 of 100 first-place votes, second-highest total behind Evan Mobley) announced him as a genuine defensive weapon. A second consecutive All-D 1st nod in 2025-26 is expected (search indicates Rockets' only award for the season). The previous library tag of "Dribble Pass Shoot Wing" was a projection-era classification; the S99 migration to Defensive Specialist (Wing) reflects what he actually does now — defensive identity primary, athleticism-first offense, broken jumper.

Offensively the profile is built on rim attack and secondary athleticism. Rim FG% 71.8% on 448 attempts weighted is elite — he converts everything at the basket. Dunk rate at 40% of rim attempts is extraordinary; 49.8% of his total shots come at the rim. Cut PPP 1.380 on 224 possessions weighted (66th-80th percentile across both seasons) shows he finishes what the offense creates. Transition PPP 1.175 on 273 possessions is strong secondary athletic profile. The problem is perimeter shooting: CAS 3PT 25.6%, pull-up 3PT 12.0%, overall 3PT% 23.6% — these aren't dips, they're structural gaps. Defenses sag off him, which limits Rockets' offensive spacing when he's on the floor. Playmaking has expanded — 5.3 APG in 2025-26 with 12.4% adjusted assist rate, A:TO 2.10 — solid but not the weapons-grade passing that would unlock a Defensive-Engine Playmaker archetype elevation. Floater 42.6% on 289 FGA is adequate touch for the archetype; FT% 75.1% weighted.

Defensively he is the identity. DFGPOE -3.8% across both seasons is elite; 1.5 STL/g and 0.9 BLK/g from a wing is rare profile; 2.8 deflections/g shows active hands and constant engagement. ISO defense PPP 0.869 (57th percentile) confirms one-on-one coverage quality. Post defense PPP 0.941 holds up on small sample. Team DRtg 109.4 with him on is legitimate (Rockets finished 52-30 #2 seed West in 2024-25). His switchability covers 1-4 guards and wings, and his athletic reach lets him contest shots without fouling.

Rebounding is a quiet elite dimension. OREB% 7.8% is exceptional for a wing (typical wing OREB% is 3-5%). Total rebounding 7.96/g on 37 MPG reflects motor and reach. Character/motor: no red flags surfaced, team-culture-first reputation, works as #2/3 option on a contending team without ego friction. The 2025-26 playoff sample (2 GP so far) is too small to read qualitatively; the 2024-25 playoff run saw the Rockets exit the first round with Amen producing 15.7 PPG on -5.7 TS% delta.

Bottom line: the drift-sweep hypothesis was that Amen's elite 2024-25 All-Defensive First Team credential + star-role minutes warranted upward drift. Confirmed. The archetype migration from Dribble Pass Shoot Wing to Defensive Specialist (Wing) is the bigger correction — the old tag never fit a non-shooter. Under S99-F01 strict R13 gate, Stage 2 does not fire (career matched TS% delta -6.2 is "strong statistical shrink" per script, but qualitative direction is limited-sample undetermined — no convergence). The credential-driven move from 8.00 to 8.20 places him at upper Tier 7 — the right spot given All-D 1st credential anchor, star-minutes trajectory, and the shooting/All-Star ceiling constraints that Tyler correctly identified as the Draymond/Barnes archetypal limit.
