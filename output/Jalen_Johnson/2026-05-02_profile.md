## 3. Scouting Narrative

A 6'9", 220-pound forward whose game has outgrown its draft-night scouting report. The first thing on tape is the size-skill collision: a wing-sized frame with a primary-creator's handle, a passer's eyes, and the kind of vertical-leverage rebounding that anchors transition offense before the offense even resets. He plays the four because of his frame, but the value driver is the way the size pairs with face-up skill — drives that start as ball-handling reps and end at the rim, kick-outs that arrive from long-distance angles, and rebounds-to-coast-to-coast attacks that shrink opposing defenses by ten feet of recovery.

Offensively, he is a high-volume rim attacker first. The interior conversion is genuine — he finishes through length with a strong off-hand and converts above the rim at a real clip. Catch-and-shoot is sustainable, not a weapon — a low-mid-30s percentage on real volume that defenses must respect but do not yet game-plan around. He is functional from the mid-range floater zone and from the foul line. The off-dribble jumper is the open hole: pull-ups generate looks but not points, and his step-back is mechanically inconsistent. The thing that ties the offense together is the playmaking. He runs primary creator load — heavy touches, heavy possessions, deep PnR volume — and turns it into a near-eight-assist line as a 6'9" forward, with court vision and pass-execution that earned a franchise triple-double record this season. The turnover number is the price of the volume; it does not look reckless, it looks usage-pressured.

Defensively, he projects as a credible multi-position defender without being a shutdown one. Length, lateral mobility, and rebounding instinct give him real switchability — he can survive on guards and hold against most wings and undersized fours. The current-season closeout numbers softened from his prior-year peak as offensive load expanded; the swing factor for projection is whether the defensive intensity scales back up when the team builds around his offense rather than through it. Help defense and post coverage are functional. Rim protection is incidental rather than core — his block rate is low for his size, but the defensive rebounding (top-of-position percentile) absorbs much of the same value: ending opposing possessions one way or another.

Motor and compete are real. Multi-source character signals — coach, trainer, peer — converge on a player who treats hard work as identity. The 2025-26 season was his first full healthy campaign since high school junior year and he closed it with thirteen triple-doubles, a first All-Star selection, and a mature post-series read after losing in six games to New York. The early-career flags — leaving IMG before his senior year, opting out at Duke — are old, self-acknowledged, and increasingly orphaned by NBA-level evidence. Trajectory is clearly upward.

The bottom line: a 24-year-old ascending All-Around Wing with playmaking-engine accents, a genuinely elite rebounding floor, and one obvious offensive gap (off-the-dribble shooting) that limits the primary-scorer ceiling but does not constrain the all-around value. He is not yet at the All-NBA level Tatum or LeBron occupy in this archetype; he is in the All-Star starter band with room to climb if the off-dribble jumper improves and the durability holds.
