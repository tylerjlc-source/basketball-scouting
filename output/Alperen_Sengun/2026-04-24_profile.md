## Section 3 — Scouting Narrative

Şengün is the modern high-post hub big — an All-Around Big in the literal Jokić/Davis/Bam mold whose value flows through the post and through his hands rather than from above the rim. The first thing you notice is the footwork: a constellation of pivots, spins, drop steps, up-and-unders, and counters that produces interior offense out of stillness rather than burst. The second thing you notice is the passing. He sees the floor like a guard, throws every angle, and runs Houston's offense from the elbow and the post in a way almost no big his age has ever done.

Offensively he is two things at once: a top-tier post scorer with elite touch on the floater (498 floater-zone attempts at 43.8%, a defining shot diet for the position) and a primary playmaker who creates more open looks for teammates than most starting point guards. The post repertoire is full — hook, spin, drop step, fade — and the catch-and-go counter game gets him to the paint at 8.2 drives per game and 54.1% conversion. He gets to the line at a 0.351 free-throw rate, above big-baseline. The shooting is the gap. Despite a public offseason rebuild with Cam Hodges and a Serbian shooting coach, the season-aggregate three-point number sits at 28-29% on low volume; the headline-grabbing early-season hot stretches did not sustain. Mid-range and free-throw conversion are both in the functional-but-not-weaponized zone. Spacing is not yet the value-add the Rockets bet it would become.

Defensively he is a contested bet. Help defense, instincts, and active hands have genuinely improved under Ime Udoka's roamer-style scheme — 2.4 deflections per game, 1.2 steals, and a career-high block rate confirm a more disruptive defender than the early-career caricature. But the structural ceilings are real and the playoffs keep exposing them. He is hunted in pick-and-roll, lacks the lateral quickness to stay in front of perimeter creators on switches, and lacks the vertical pop to recover into rim-protection geometry. He passes the center non-negotiable on rim protection by a marginal half-point. He does not project as a paint anchor. He is a help-and-rebound defender whose value on that end depends on scheme architecture around him.

The character read is positive without being defining. Two consecutive All-Star nods, the EuroBasket 2025 silver and near-MVP run with Türkiye, year-round availability, and Udoka's "perennial guy" public backing all add up. The counter-signal is the playoff tape: a career matched −7.7 TS% delta over nine games and two series, the 0-2 hole opening this 2025-26 first round vs. the Lakers, and "inefficient playoffs" language across multiple cycles. The pattern is real; the sample is also small. He is competing through a stress moment that hasn't fully resolved.

The bottom line: a high-floor, high-ceiling offensive hub at center whose modeling problem is a defensive structural cap and a playoff signal that says the regular-season rubric flatters his current reality. The 8.45 composite reflects clear All-Star — perhaps perennially — without yet pushing into the All-NBA tier, because the path there runs through either rim-protection emergence (unlikely given athletic profile) or playoff efficiency that matches his regular-season hub production. The Jokić aspirational ceiling is the right comparison frame, but the realistic landing is lower — closer to a higher-volume Sabonis with better vision.
