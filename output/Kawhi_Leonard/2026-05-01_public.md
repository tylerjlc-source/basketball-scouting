# Kawhi Leonard — Public Profile

## Identity

At 34, Kawhi Leonard is a Score-First Wing operating on craft and engineered footwork rather than the explosive two-way force he was at his peak. He stands 6'7" with a 7'3" wingspan, around 225 pounds, and carries hands large enough to swallow the ball. The credentials anchor a generational competitive resume: 2x Finals MVP, 2x Defensive Player of the Year, 7x All-Star, 6x All-NBA. Multi-season knee management has recalibrated his athletic baseline. Leonard's ceiling is limited, but he is still an elite two-way threat.

## Strength

Offensively, Leonard is one of the league's most efficient half-court scorers. At this stage in his career, his game centers around this proficiency. The mid-range is the signature: 46.8% on 215 attempts at a career-high 26.6% diet share, with footwork that gets the 15-footer off against any defender. Catch-and-shoot three is automatic at 40.3%, the pull-up jumper holds at 37.4% from deep with a 50.0% pull-up mid-range, and the free throw is 87.9%. He runs primary creator at 30.8% usage, generates an 84th-percentile pick-and-roll attack, and turns drives into 50% conversion with elite ball security on 10.2 drives per game.

## Weakness

Defensively the picture is honest: functional, not elite. Opposing teams shoot 2.9% above expected against him as the closest defender. He has not made an All-Defensive team since 2021 and has admitted directly that he is no longer at the same level; the sustained product is solid coverage, not the lockdown wing he was at his peak. Help defense is the same shape: he handles his individual matchup but does not anchor team rotations the way he did in San Antonio or Toronto. Playmaking is the other limiter, with assists functional rather than engine-level at 30.8% usage. Strength remains a structural elite trait while burst and lateral quickness sit at solid-wing baseline now.

## Projection-verdict

Leonard is a closing-window star operating on craft and credential. The realistic ceiling is sustained All-Star presence with All-NBA reach if the 2024-25 first-round form against Denver, 25 points per game on 63% true shooting across seven, sustains alongside continued 2025-26 durability. The realistic floor is a closer's role rather than a primary engine if chronic-knee management accelerates the decline. The 2014 and 2019 Finals MVP version is gone, but the mid-range and free-throw engine plus the generational competitive resume keep him productive through this final cycle.
