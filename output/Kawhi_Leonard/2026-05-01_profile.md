## Section 3 — Scouting Narrative

Kawhi Leonard at 34 is a Score-First Wing operating on craft and engineered footwork rather than the explosive two-way force he was at peak. The first thing you notice is the engineering: economy of movement, balance through contact, hands that swallow the ball, and a 7'3" wingspan that creates cushion against any closeout. The frame is intact — he is still too strong to screen and too long to elude — but the burst is no longer above-rim, and the lateral recovery he depended on as a 2x DPOY is no longer the bedrock of his defensive identity. Multi-season knee management has recalibrated his athletic baseline; this is the player now, not a temporary suppression.

Offensively he is one of the league's most efficient half-court scorers and the engine of the profile. The mid-range is the signature — career-high diet share at 26.6% of attempts, 47% conversion on heavy volume, with footwork that gets the 15-footer off against any defender. The catch-and-shoot three is automatic at 40% on real volume; the pull-up jumper is reliable at 37% from deep with a 50% pull-up mid; the free throw is elite. He runs primary creator at 30.8% USG, generates an 84th-percentile pick-and-roll attack, and turns drives into 50% shooting with elite ball security. Touch is a foundation — 51.7% on a 360-attempt floater diet, 87.9% from the line. The playmaking is the limiter: he reads what is in front of him cleanly, executes within his range, but does not collapse defenses with anticipatory passing — assists are functional, not engine-level.

Defensively the picture is honest: functional, not elite. Opposing teams shoot 2.9% above expected against him as the closest defender. He has not made an All-Defensive team since 2021 and has admitted directly that he is no longer at the same level. The flashes are real — 5 STL and 3 BLK in the 55-point game against Detroit, the "apex predator" Game 2 vs Denver in the 2024-25 first round — but the sustained product is solid coverage, not the lockdown wing he was at peak. Help defense is the same shape: he handles his individual matchup but does not anchor team rotations the way he did in San Antonio or Toronto. Strength remains a structural elite trait, and post defense holds up in switch situations against wings.

Character is the floor of the profile. The credentials anchor a generational competitive resume — 2x Finals MVP (2014, 2019), 2x DPOY, 7x All-Star, 6x All-NBA — and the playoff record across 12 seasons and 146 games is a +1.6 TS% career rise over regular-season baseline. The 2024-25 first-round series against Denver was a "Legend of Playoff Kawhi" reprise: 25 PPG on 63% TS across seven games, with a 39-point closeout-quality Game 2 that re-anchored the league-wide framing. The most recent data point cuts the other way — the 2026 play-in elimination loss to Golden State (full participation, 8-of-17 shooting, 1-of-6 from three) is the most recent worst-observation, and it ended the season before the conference round. He shows up in big moments more than he disappears, but the body of recent evidence is mixed.

Bottom line: a complete profile that has compressed at the top. Twenty-two of twenty-six sub-domains score at 7.0 or above; zero land below 5.0. The signature mid-range and free-throw engine plus heavy credential weight place him at Tier 4 floor. The defensive erosion and athletic recalibration are real and price into the placement; the 65 healthy games this season and career-high 27.9 PPG counter the chronic-injury concern but do not lift the ceiling beyond where the rubric anchors it. He is a current All-NBA candidate operating below his peak, with a closing-window outcome distribution.

## How to read these ratings

A complete basketball player is built from 26 attributes — the specific skills, physical tools, and intangibles that determine ability on the court. Every player is rated on every attribute.

- **Attribute ratings (1–10)** grade each individual attribute — like finishing at the rim, off-ball defense, or shot selection — against other players at the same position. A 9 in shooting for a guard means elite among guards.
- **Skill area ratings (1–10)** organize the 26 attributes into broader areas like Shooting, Defense, and Playmaking, giving a faster read of a player's profile without the full detail.
- **Overall player grade (OVR, 1–10)** grades a player's complete basketball ability against the entire basketball universe. A 10.00 would be a theoretically perfect player; around 6.79 marks the floor for NBA-caliber play. Position, height, weight, and archetype do not change where a player lands — every player is measured on the same scale.

The OVR is not calculated from the attribute ratings or skill areas. It is assigned by comparing the player against a library of anchor players whose ratings have been calibrated and locked in.

That is why an OVR can sit higher than any individual skill area. The OVR measures the whole player against the league; the attribute ratings and skill areas show where specific strengths and weaknesses fall within position.
