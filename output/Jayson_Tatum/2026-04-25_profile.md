## Section 3 — Scouting Narrative

The first thing you notice about Tatum is the leverage. At 6'8" with a 6'11" wingspan and a 210-pound filled-out frame, he creates a profile most wings can't match — long enough to shoot over closeouts at any angle, strong enough to switch onto bigs in the post, balanced enough to stay in front of guards laterally. He's not the most explosive wing in the league; he's not Giannis, Kawhi-prime, or Edwards as a leaper. What he is, instead, is one of the most distributed elite-tier two-way wing profiles the league has seen — a player whose value is built through length, footwork, decision speed, and skill at every position on the floor.

Offensively, Tatum lives at every level. The catch-and-shoot three is his foundation skill — healthy-anchor 40% on real volume, nearly 380 attempts in a 60/40 weighted window, weapon-status from both corners and above-the-break. He's not the elite pull-up shooter; pull-up threes sit at league-average around 33%, isolation efficiency dragged below his hot-stretch press clippings to a full-season 39th-percentile mark. The mid-range game has been recalibrated through Drew Hanlen's footwork work — long-twos compressed, shot quality improved. Where Tatum has genuinely leveled up is the passing. He averaged a career-high six assists in the 2024-25 healthy season and led Boston in points, rebounds, and assists through the 2024 Finals, becoming the first Celtic ever with a 30-point, 10-assist Finals game. He sees second-level reads, delivers pinpoint passes over one defender or two, and protects the ball at the volume of a primary handler — A:TO 2.13, low TOV at 29% USG. The handle isn't elite — ISO PPP doesn't approach SGA or Harden — but the touch (43.6% on 130 floater attempts; 81.6% FT; reliable passing accuracy) elevates plays that pure athletes can't.

The defense is what makes him an All-Around Wing rather than a Score-First Wing or Dribble-Pass-Shoot Wing. Tatum is genuinely switchable — opponents shoot 4.3% worse than expected against him on the season (DFGPOE −4.3% wt), and he held a 6'10" starting center to 31.6% in a recent playoff possession sample. He's not a primary rim defender — 0.3 BPG is below the wing rim-protection bar — and the 6.0 score there reflects that he doesn't anchor the basket. But he rotates correctly in Boston's elite scheme, posts a +1.8 DBPM that sits at career-high in his post-Achilles return, and pulls down 27% of available defensive rebounds (career-high 2025-26) at rates approaching big-tier. That rebounding number is one of the strongest in the wing anchor library — a genuine primary skill, not a peripheral contribution.

The character profile is the multiplier. Four straight All-NBA First Team selections, including a unanimous 100/100 vote in 2025. A championship in 2024 in which he led the team in points, rebounds, and assists. A Twyman-Stokes Teammate of the Year finalist nod. A historic-pace Achilles recovery built on three-a-day work — back on the floor in 10 months, ramping into 25-point playoff games by April 2026. The "most scrutinized player in the playoffs" critique cycle that followed him through 2024 was answered by a Game 5 closeout: 31 points, 11 assists, championship parade. Career playoff TS% delta is −1.6, neutral band — not the "playoff disappear" pattern critics framed, just normal star-level efficiency drag.

The bottom line: Tatum sits at the top of Tier 5 with a profile that pre-injury would have been climbing through Tier 4. The 8.88 reflects current state — Achilles recovery is in active proof-of-return mode with strong trajectory, but full healthy peak hasn't been re-demonstrated yet. He's at the boundary between "perennial All-Star" and "All-NBA caliber clear franchise piece" — and the next healthy season is likely to push him cleanly through.

## How to read these ratings

A complete basketball player is built from 26 attributes — the specific skills, physical tools, and intangibles that determine ability on the court. Every player is rated on every attribute.

- **Attribute ratings (1–10)** grade each individual attribute — like finishing at the rim, off-ball defense, or shot selection — against other players at the same position. A 9 in shooting for a guard means elite among guards.
- **Skill area ratings (1–10)** organize the 26 attributes into broader areas like Shooting, Defense, and Playmaking, giving a faster read of a player's profile without the full detail.
- **Overall player grade (OVR, 1–10)** grades a player's complete basketball ability against the entire basketball universe. A 10.00 would be a theoretically perfect player; around 6.79 marks the floor for NBA-caliber play. Position, height, weight, and archetype do not change where a player lands — every player is measured on the same scale.

The OVR is not calculated from the attribute ratings or skill areas. It is assigned by comparing the player against a library of anchor players whose ratings have been calibrated and locked in.

That is why an OVR can sit higher than any individual skill area. The OVR measures the whole player against the league; the attribute ratings and skill areas show where specific strengths and weaknesses fall within position.
