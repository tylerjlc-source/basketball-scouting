# Jayson Tatum — Public Profile

## Identity

Jayson Tatum is one of the most distributed elite two-way wings in the league. A 4x All-NBA First Team selection (2022-2025, unanimous in 2025), a 6x All-Star, and the 2024 NBA Champion who led Boston in points, rebounds, and assists through the title run. At 6'8" with a 6'11" wingspan and a 210-pound filled-out frame, he carries the leverage to shoot over closeouts at any angle, switch onto bigs in the post, and stay in front of guards laterally. Not the most explosive wing in the league; instead, a player whose value is built through length, footwork, decision speed, and skill at every position on the floor.

## Strength

Offensively, Tatum lives at every level. The catch-and-shoot three is his foundation skill: an elite 40.0% on 377 weighted attempts over the last 2 seasons, a weapon from both corners and above the break. Where he has genuinely leveled up is the passing: a career-high 6.0 assists per game in 2024-25 and 7.2 per game leading the 2024 Finals, capped by a 31-point, 11-assist Game 5 closeout to clinch the title. He sees second-level reads and protects the ball on primary-handler volume with a 2.13 assist-to-turnover ratio at 29.4% usage. Defensively, Tatum is genuinely switchable: opponents shoot 4.3% worse than expected against him on the season, he posts a career-high +1.8 defensive box plus-minus on the 2025-26 return, and the defensive glass is a real asset at a 27% defensive rebound rate during 2025-26, approaching big-position anchor rates.

## Weakness

Where Tatum is not elite is in the self-creation pull-up game and at the rim defensively. The pull-up three sits at league-average around 32.9% in 2024-25, and isolation efficiency at 0.806 points per possession lands at the 39th percentile, well short of the elite handler tier. Tatum is not a primary rim defender either: he posts 0.3 blocks per game, below the wing rim-protection bar, and his defensive value comes from pressure, switchability, and rotations within Boston's elite scheme rather than basket deterrence. The third gap is offensive rebounding at a 1.6% rate since 2024-25, but that reflects the demands of his primary-scorer role rather than a skill weakness.

## Projection-verdict

Tatum sits at the threshold between perennial All-Star and the league's MVP candidates, with the next healthy season the likely tipping point. The realistic ceiling is a return to the pre-Achilles form that had him climbing toward the MVP / Finals MVP conversation, with another deep playoff run as Boston's roster regenerates around him. The realistic floor is a partial-recovery skill-vet path, where intact size, skill, and feel continue to produce All-Star regular seasons but the pre-injury peak does not return. What he is, year over year, is a star wing with the physical tools to keep producing into his 30s. What he is not, yet, is the player who has cleared the MVP threshold; a full healthy season is what stands between him and that line.
