## 3. Scouting narrative

LeBron James at 41, third option on the Doncic Lakers, 23rd NBA season, four championships and four Finals MVPs in the rear view. The body is still the same imposing 6'9", 250-lb wing frame that has set the era's wing template — frame intact where the explosion is not. The first thing on tape is what hasn't changed: the read-and-react vision, the passing arrival timing, the willingness to take the right shot rather than the available shot, the quiet authority of someone who has organized winning offenses in 23 different NBA seasons.

Offense in 2025-26 runs through Doncic, and James has accepted the recasting — career-low usage at 24.2%, 22 PPG (lowest since rookie year), 9.4 PnR/game as ball-handler down from 19.5 two years ago. What works inside the shrunken role: rim finishing remains elite (75.1% on 358 weighted FGA, dunk rate 23.4% confirms above-rim access), playmaking still drives the offense when Doncic sits (7.6 weighted AST, 13.0 potential AST, 19.1 AST points created — top-10 wing creation tier), and the cut/transition reads convert at high pctile (Cut 1.39 PPP, Transition 1.26 PPP at 82nd pctile, 26.7% transition frequency). What doesn't: the jumper. Catch-and-shoot 3PT collapsed from 42.0% in 2024-25 to 32.8% this year; pull-up 3PT 31.9% weighted is well below average; mid-range 42.0% is solid not elite. The shot diet reads honest — early-clock and transition attempts dominate (43% in average-clock zone, only 10% in late-clock), correct sequencing given the pull-up reliability question. Off-dribble shot creation is no longer the weapon; off-ball reaction-spacer + secondary creator + scaled-back primary engine when needed is the current configuration.

Defense splits cleanly into two modes. Regular-season engagement is variable to liability — multi-source framing through 2025-26 ("defensive liability" / "subpar perimeter defenders"); Lakers' record reportedly better without him through the November sciatica window. Playoff engagement is genuinely elite — 2025 postseason analytics framed him as the most dominant rim protector and versatile defender on the floor; 2026 first-round vs Houston averaging 1.5 STL/g + active rim contests + the Game 3 OT win line of 29/13/6/3 stl/1 blk in 45 minutes. The two-mode pattern is the post-peak vet's energy economy — coast through the long regular season to bank the playoff body. Underneath it: defensive IQ and length are intact (help defense 7.5, post defense 7.0, ISO defense PPP 0.503 at 94th–99th pctile when targeted); lateral quickness and recovery athleticism have meaningfully diminished (avg defensive speed 3.39 mph below NBA wing average; on-ball pressure capped at 6.0 by lateral). Switchability remains adequate against bigs in playoff settings via size + the still-elite 250-lb strength frame; guards in space exploit the lateral gap when he is hunted in PnR coverage.

The character read isn't a question — 4× champion, 4× Finals MVP (only Jordan more), most clutch shots since 1997 (NBA record by 2× margin), 41 career playoff series wins (NBA record), all-time scoring leader regular season AND playoffs, 23 seasons without a public character flag. The trajectory is unambiguously declining — every primary scoring metric down year-over-year, public retirement language ("could very well be" his last season), first opening-night absence of his career (sciatica through November). What holds: cognitive processing, decision-making, vision, clutch composure (clutch FG% 55.3%, +/- +1.7 across 80 minutes 2-season), competitive engagement at high stakes. What's gone: the every-night offensive engine identity, the lock-in defensive engagement across all 82 games, the recovery athleticism that allowed him to compensate for tactical errors. The career arc is in its tail — the question is when, not whether.

This is the rare evaluation where the active-current rating sits four tiers below the prior career-median rating (8.95 active vs prior 9.88 retired-tagged). The library was carrying him at All-Time Top-3 status — accurate for career median, indefensible for current 2025-26 production. As an active vet at 41, the profile is Tier 4 lower-middle: All-NBA caliber when fully engaged (playoff form, 2026 first-round confirms), accepting tertiary role gracefully in regular season, declining shooting and athletic ceiling beneath an intact playmaking + IQ + character core. He sits below Doncic (current peak primary engine), at par with Cade-ascending, above the deflated/scoring-guard cluster (Edwards / Booker / Mitchell / Kawhi). Forward-looking: POT below composite reflects 2/3 post-peak conditions firing; Min/Max wide reflects the year-over-year retirement-window uncertainty.

## How to read these ratings

A complete basketball player is built from 26 attributes — the specific skills, physical tools, and intangibles that determine ability on the court. Every player is rated on every attribute.

- **Attribute ratings (1–10)** grade each individual attribute — like finishing at the rim, off-ball defense, or shot selection — against other players at the same position. A 9 in shooting for a guard means elite among guards.
- **Skill area ratings (1–10)** organize the 26 attributes into broader areas like Shooting, Defense, and Playmaking, giving a faster read of a player's profile without the full detail.
- **Overall player grade (OVR, 1–10)** grades a player's complete basketball ability against the entire basketball universe. A 10.00 would be a theoretically perfect player; around 6.79 marks the floor for NBA-caliber play. Position, height, weight, and archetype do not change where a player lands — every player is measured on the same scale.

The OVR is not calculated from the attribute ratings or skill areas. It is assigned by comparing the player against a library of anchor players whose ratings have been calibrated and locked in.

That is why an OVR can sit higher than any individual skill area. The OVR measures the whole player against the league; the attribute ratings and skill areas show where specific strengths and weaknesses fall within position.
