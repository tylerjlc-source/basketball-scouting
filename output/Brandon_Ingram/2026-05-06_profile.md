## 3. SCOUTING NARRATIVE

Brandon Ingram is a Score-First Wing whose identity has hardened around mid-range volume scoring on a long, lean frame. The 6'8" / 7'3" wingspan is the structural advantage and the structural ceiling at once — high-release, length-driven shotmaking is the engine, and 190 pounds at 28 sets the floor on what defense and post-game ever become. Ten years in, the picture is clean: a 2x All-Star scoring wing on a $40M-per-year deal whose offense is genuine and whose two-way ceiling has been priced into anchor placement long enough that the projection lives or dies on what playoff cycles say in the next three seasons.

The offensive profile is dense and stacked toward the mid-range. He took 31% of his 2025-26 attempts from the floater zone (4–14 ft) and converted 46.9% on 291 shots — calling-card territory, with elbow pulls (53.8% from 22–18 ft) and baseline pull-ups both functional. Catch-and-shoot threes are a real weapon at 40.5% across both eval-window seasons on heavy volume; the high release point and quick gather translate clean. The pull-up three is the offensive pocket — 29.6% on real volume is the avoidable shot, and shot-selection discipline rather than mechanics is the fix. As a primary handler he turns 61.3 touches per game and 11.1 drives per game into a workable but uneven creator profile: ISO is mid-pack (51.9 percentile), pick-and-roll ball-handler is genuinely below average (39.1 percentile), and drives terminate in pull-ups more often than power finishes through contact. Secondary playmaking is real but not elevating — 4.3 APG / 8.3 potential AST / 14.6% Adj AST% on 28% USG fits a "scoring gravity opens looks" profile rather than a true second-creator who reshapes possessions. The Raptors-system data captures the shape directly: cuts at 1.347 PPP and second-most off-screen volume in the NBA, but off-screen efficiency at 0.944 PPP because nearly every screen produces a catch-survey-isolation rather than a clean shot.

Defense is the priced-in liability. Lateral quickness has been a multi-source concern since the 2016 draft and has not improved; on-ball footwork is below NBA-starter standard, DFGPOE at +2.1% means the team gives up modestly more than expected when he's the primary defender, and the 7'3" wingspan converts to 0.7 BPG — length without verticality or lateral discipline. Help defense is the same shape — DEF rating 115.3, hustle production thin (0.30 def box-outs, 0.16 def loose balls, zero charges drawn). Strength caps the rest: 190 pounds means switches onto bigger wings collapse quickly and the post game on either end is a near-zero. The 5.0–5.5 cluster across on-ball, help, post defense, lateral, and strength isn't an effort accident — it's the structural floor under the long lean frame.

The character read is asymmetric. Skill-development work ethic is multi-source elite — "true professional," "second to none," teammate consensus on early arrivals and craft refinement. In-game competitive engagement is the gap: defensive disengagement and off-ball movement that lags volume are documented in the same multi-source frame. The competitive-character read is anchored by the playoff shrink pattern — career matched-season TS% delta of −5.3 across 3 series, with two consecutive recent shrink runs (−12.8 vs OKC 2024, −13.3 vs CLE 2026) and multi-source qualitative convergence ("M.I.A.", "offense slowed when on floor"). The lone counterweight is the 2022 NOR carry run (+3.0 TS delta over 6 games), which sits four years back. Trajectory at 28 is plateau — the 2025-26 healthy-bookend 77 GP / 21.5 PPG year matched career-median production and earned a Curry-replacement All-Star nod in February, but the May playoff exit confirmed rather than reset the league-wide narrative.

Bottom line: Ingram lives at the upper edge of Tier 7 because the mid-range is genuine, the 2x All-Star credential is durable, and the healthy bookend year delivered. He doesn't push into Tier 6 because the playoff shrink is now the third instance in five years, the defensive structural floor is priced in, and the 2026 cycle's "ball moved more freely without him" narrative is now public scouting language. The realistic ceiling is "return to demonstrated peak" — not exceed it.
