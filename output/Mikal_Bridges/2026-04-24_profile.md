## Section 3 — Scouting Narrative

Bridges is the template 3-and-D Wing of the current era with a specific profile-shape signature: starter-volume production on a contender, iron-man durability, high-volume catch-and-shoot shooting, and a defensive reputation that outpaces current on-court data. He's played 555+ consecutive games entering the 2025-26 season, led the NBA in total minutes in 2024-25 (3,036), and started all 82 games in both of the past two regular seasons. At 6'6" with a 7'1" wingspan, the length is real. The two-way contribution is real. What the advanced data and multi-year track record add is honest calibration: the defense is above-average not elite, the offense is efficient-role-player not primary-weapon, and the playoff ceiling is lower than the regular-season reputation suggests.

Offensively he functions as a high-volume spot-up shooter with elite finishing touch on the interior. The CAS 3PT profile is stable at 37% on 437 weighted 3PAs — real volume, real translation to team spacing, with particular strength from the corner (41.2%). Around the rim he converts 74.7% on moderate volume, and his non-dunk finishing at 71.5% reflects elite touch despite a modest 14.7% dunk rate — length over vertical, finishing via angles and reach rather than explosion. The floater game is a genuine signature: 49.4% on 312 weighted attempts (nearly 30% of his shot diet) is top-tier wing volume-efficiency. What the offense doesn't have is self-creation. Pull-up 3PT sits at 28.7%, ISO PPP at 54th percentile, and PnR BH at 47th percentile — he doesn't break defenses down off the dribble and never has. Contact avoidance is structural: FTR 0.101 is bottom-10 in the NBA among perimeter scorers. He gets to the line once in every 10 field-goal attempts, which for a 6'6" wing playing 33-37 MPG is a real limiter on scoring ceiling. The 2025-26 regular-season scoring drop from 17.6 to 14.7 PPG under new HC Mike Brown is a noted inconsistency theme but not a mechanical break — the shooting percentages held.

Defensively the profile is where reputation and data diverge most sharply. Bridges finished 2nd in DPOY voting in 2021-22 with Phoenix, earning a reputation as an elite multi-position perimeter defender who could shut down the league's best scoring wings. Current data complicates the reputation: DFGPOE sits at −0.4% across the 2-season weighted window, which is just above average, not elite. ISO Def PPP at 0.748 is elite on the thin 1.8-possession weighted sample, and the clutch-minutes pairing with OG Anunoby produces a 96.6 team DRtg — genuine signals that the defense still rises in high-stakes moments. The 2024-25 ECF run vs. Boston produced real defensive heroics: a game-winning steal in Game 1 OT, and a league-best 5 stocks in the final 10 seconds of one-possession games, with two of those plays directly sealing Games 1 and 2 in Boston. But the body of evidence across eight years shows a defender whose regular-season impact is solid-above-average rather than the elite-stopper framing — 2022 playoffs he allowed Brandon Ingram 36 points on 13-of-21 / 6-of-8 3PT as the primary defender, and the Valley of the Suns post-mortem "How the Phoenix Suns lost confidence in their defensive stud" captured the reputational turn.

Character sourcing is positive and durable. The iron-man streak isn't accidental — it traces to Jay Wright's Villanova availability philosophy and a work ethic documented consistently across the Phoenix, Brooklyn, and New York stops. There are no off-court flags, no character concerns, no locker-room incidents across eight years and three franchises. The durability itself is a genuine value driver that league hardware doesn't capture — he's been a 37-MPG iron man while accumulating zero load-management absences. What tempers the character read is the playoff signal: across five qualifying playoff runs (60 games), Bridges has posted a career TS% delta of −5.1 vs regular-season baseline — statistically strong shrink, with three of his three most-recent runs all showing meaningful shrink (2022-23 PHX/BKN −4.8, 2024-25 NYK ECF −6.7, 2025-26 NYK current −12.8). The 2025-26 series vs Atlanta has produced current struggles including a 0-point, 0-for-3 Game 3 described as "Tony Snell-esque" by regional coverage.

Bottom line: Bridges is a solid starting NBA wing whose role volume and durability exceed his credential stack. Tier 8 upper third — Quality-contributor-on-contender territory — is where the composite settles after R13 Stage 2 Moderate Shrink is applied to account for the active, multi-run playoff shrink pattern. The regular-season profile is materially above the specialist role players in the lower Tier 8 band (Caruso, Jones, Claxton, Pritchard) and sits one credential-tier below the hardware-anchored Tier 7 floor (Smart, Anunoby). This is a re-evaluation that validates Tyler's calibration hypothesis — the prior 7.62 anchor under-weighted starter-role-volume on a contender team — and corrects upward to 7.85.

## How to read these ratings

A complete basketball player is built from 26 attributes — the specific skills, physical tools, and intangibles that determine ability on the court. Every player is rated on every attribute.

- **Attribute ratings (1–10)** grade each individual attribute — like finishing at the rim, off-ball defense, or shot selection — against other players at the same position. A 9 in shooting for a guard means elite among guards.
- **Skill area ratings (1–10)** organize the 26 attributes into broader areas like Shooting, Defense, and Playmaking, giving a faster read of a player's profile without the full detail.
- **Overall player grade (OVR, 1–10)** grades a player's complete basketball ability against the entire basketball universe. A 10.00 would be a theoretically perfect player; around 6.79 marks the floor for NBA-caliber play. Position, height, weight, and archetype do not change where a player lands — every player is measured on the same scale.

The OVR is not calculated from the attribute ratings or skill areas. It is assigned by comparing the player against a library of anchor players whose ratings have been calibrated and locked in.

That is why an OVR can sit higher than any individual skill area. The OVR measures the whole player against the league; the attribute ratings and skill areas show where specific strengths and weaknesses fall within position.
