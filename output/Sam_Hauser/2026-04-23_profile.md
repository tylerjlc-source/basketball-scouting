Hauser is a pure Shooting Specialist — the Celtics' floor-spacing outlet on a roster anchored by two gravitational scorers, a 6'7" wing whose entire offensive value flows through one elite skill delivered via quick release, mechanical reliability, and constant off-ball motion. The system's archetype doc names Davis Bertans, Ryan Anderson, and Kyle Korver as his confirmed peers — Hauser is a proof-of-concept modern Shooting Specialist who earned his market via sustained 41%+ 3PT over a five-year career.

Offensively the profile is narrow and excellent. Three primary archetype value drivers are all anchored: catch-and-shoot elite (8.3), off-ball movement solid-to-elite on genuine volume (7.6 — 70 OffScreen and 124 Transition weighted possessions, 4.88 mph off-ball speed leading this batch), and shot selection above-average (7.7). Rim finishing is 77.7% weighted — elite efficiency on low volume (5.5% of shots), which is archetype-appropriate, not a lack of capability. 92.6% assisted rate on rim finishes tells the full story: nearly every interior touch comes off ball movement, not self-creation. Ball security at 8.1 is elite for any archetype — TOV per 100 touches at 1.3, A:TO 3.07 — and touch/feel at 8.2 confirms the mechanics are clean at every scale. Creation is structurally absent: ISO 0 possessions, USG 14.1%, near-zero drive volume. He doesn't get to the line (FTR 0.029) and doesn't need to.

Defensively, he's a genuinely-improving scheme-fit. DFGPOE sat at +4.7% two seasons ago and improved to −0.7% current season — neutral on direct matchups. Head coach Joe Mazzulla's on-record quote is the high-trust signal: "very, very good defender ... I feel just as comfortable with him guarding as anybody else." The physical tools are modest — 6'9" wingspan is only +2" over height, 217 lbs on a 6'7" frame is wing-median — but the technique and scheme awareness have developed multi-year. He's not a stopper and the switchability is scheme-dependent, not physical-dominance. Help rotation metrics (0.3 BLK/g, 1.3 deflections/g) are functional-not-elite. The trajectory is the positive signal.

Character is clean across five NBA seasons. Undrafted 2021 to 4yr/$45M extension in 2024 is the market validation; 78 games in 2025-26 plus 71 prior reflects top-tier availability; zero documented motor critiques or character flags across multi-source search; 2024 NBA Champion with genuine rotation contribution (Finals 15.4 MPG, 8.2 PPG, 47.8% 3PT, team-high +17 +/-). His 2024 Finals performance offset that pattern; the subsequent 2024-25 postseason (-5.9 delta on 8 games) reinforced it. Playoff shrink is the gap between his regular-season value delivery and his championship-stage consistency.

The bottom line is an elite-mechanics Shooting Specialist whose fit with the Celtics — floor-spacer alongside Brown and Tatum's scoring gravity — has optimized his visible role into a marginal-starter role that earned him a 4-year starter-tier contract. Projection going forward is continued rotation-starter value at 39-41% 3PT volume on a contender, with the ceiling bounded by archetype narrowness (All-Star outcomes aren't plausible for pure Shooting Specialists) and the floor bounded by the R13 shrink history (playoff role compressions have happened and could continue). Age 28 is prime years; the identity is established, the role is validated, and the trajectory is flat-with-mild-mean-reversion-risk on the shot. Best case: sustained 41% starter season in an expanded role. Worst case: role compression in a roster overhaul if the 2025-26 mild regression continues.

## How to read these ratings

A complete basketball player is built from 26 attributes — the specific skills, physical tools, and intangibles that determine ability on the court. Every player is rated on every attribute.

- **Attribute ratings (1–10)** grade each individual attribute — like finishing at the rim, off-ball defense, or shot selection — against other players at the same position. A 9 in shooting for a guard means elite among guards.
- **Skill area ratings (1–10)** organize the 26 attributes into broader areas like Shooting, Defense, and Playmaking, giving a faster read of a player's profile without the full detail.
- **Overall player grade (OVR, 1–10)** grades a player's complete basketball ability against the entire basketball universe. A 10.00 would be a theoretically perfect player; around 6.79 marks the floor for NBA-caliber play. Position, height, weight, and archetype do not change where a player lands — every player is measured on the same scale.

The OVR is not calculated from the attribute ratings or skill areas. It is assigned by comparing the player against a library of anchor players whose ratings have been calibrated and locked in.

That is why an OVR can sit higher than any individual skill area. The OVR measures the whole player against the league; the attribute ratings and skill areas show where specific strengths and weaknesses fall within position.
