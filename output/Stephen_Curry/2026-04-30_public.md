# Stephen Curry — Public Profile

## Identity

Stephen Curry is the realized ceiling of the perimeter shot creator. The credentials run as deep as anyone in his generation: 4 championships, 2 Most Valuable Player awards including the only unanimous selection in league history, a 2022 Finals MVP, and the NBA's all-time leader in career 3-pointers made. At 6'2", 185 pounds, with a 6'4" wingspan, he is built slim for the position and has carried seventeen seasons through meticulous craft. The question is no longer ascension. It is how the late-career window closes on one of the best point guards to have played the game.

## Strength

Offensively, Curry is the historical reference point at his position. The off-ball gravity is the dimension no peer has matched, the running, the relocating, the universal recognition as the best movement shooter of all time, all stitched into a half-court offense that warps defenses before he touches the ball. The shooting carries the same essence across every form: catch-and-shoot, pull-up off the bounce, dribble-handoff finish, anchored by a career free-throw percentage of 91.2% that completes the three-level profile. The signature handle extends the creation tree to isolation, pick-and-roll, and dribble-handoff weaponry. At 38 he is still producing at one of the highest scoring rates ever for a player that age.

## Weakness

Defensively, Curry is the structural limit on the profile. The slim point-guard frame is the issue: a career-long matchup target whom opposing teams isolate every playoff series. The athleticism floor of balanced burst, middling lateral quickness, and slim-frame strength has always capped the defensive ceiling, and team scheme has done the load-bearing work to keep him survivable rather than exposed. Help defense reads better than on-ball, hands and anticipation working where length cannot. At 38, knee management adds a layer of late-career limit that scheme cannot scrub away.

## Projection-verdict

Curry has already authored the verdict on his career: four championships, two Most Valuable Player awards including the only unanimous selection in league history, a 2022 Finals MVP earned without a co-superstar, the all-time record for career 3-pointers, and an All-Star fixture across the past dozen seasons. On full availability, the ceiling is a vintage swan-song with another deep playoff run and All-NBA reach. The floor is a sharper late-career decline as knee management compounds. The remaining question is whether he closes with one more championship or a quiet bow.
