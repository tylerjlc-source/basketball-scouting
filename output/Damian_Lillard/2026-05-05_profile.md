## SECTION 3 — SCOUTING NARRATIVE

Damian Lillard is a 35-year-old Offensive Engine point guard recovering from a torn left Achilles. Compact 6'2" frame with a 6'8" wingspan, well-proportioned guard build — never an elite athlete by combine measurement, always a craft and burst operator on the perimeter. Nine-time All-Star, seven-time All-NBA, owner of one of the most identifiable signatures in modern basketball: the deep pull-up three from genuinely uncomfortable distances. The first thing on tape is the gravity — defenders chasing past midcourt, the floor stretching to make room, possessions ending in a step-back from twenty-eight feet that nobody else in the league attempts at this volume.

The off-dribble pull-up is the engine. Pick-and-roll ball-handler PPP at the 89th percentile across the eval window, with the signature step-back creating real separation against on-ball pressure rather than cosmetic space. Catch-and-shoot threes converted at 38% on 560-plus attempts; pull-up threes at 36% on extreme volume; mid-range at 45% on real footwork. Free throws at 92%, automatic. Drives convert at 53% with a foul rate that still rates near the top of the guard pool despite the 2021 rule-change era depressing his line trips. The playmaking is real but secondary — seven-plus assists per game on twelve-plus potential assists, gravity-driven reads more than traditional point-guard organization. Touch and feel are excellent, ball security is strong for a 27% usage primary creator. Where elite peers separate is range of advantage creation against scheme attention; Lillard's profile gets compressed when defenses load specifically against him, which the 2024 and 2025 Indiana series both demonstrated within a single half of basketball.

Defense is a genuine liability and was so before the injury. Multi-source pre-Achilles documentation is convergent: "borderline disgraceful at times" through 2023-24, "fake hustling in the backcourt," "zero disruption as a trailing pick-and-roll defender." Bucks team defensive rating dropped from fourth in the league to nineteenth in his first year, recovered to twelfth in his second only because Doc Rivers reorganized the starting lineup specifically to mitigate him — Andre Jackson Jr. moved into the starting unit explicitly to share defensive load. Lateral quickness was never elite by combine measurement and at 33–34 had eroded further. Help-side rotations are inconsistent, screen navigation is poor, on-ball pressure is exploited by quicker guards. Rebounding lands above guard average via long uncontested defensive boards in outlet position — the sample of contested defensive rebounds is small.

Competitive character on the offensive end is anchor-grade and longitudinal. Most game-winners in the league since 2012 (ten with score within five in the final five seconds), most clutch threes in that span, multiple iconic playoff performances — the 2014 Game 6 buzzer-beater over Houston, the 2019 thirty-seven-foot wave-goodbye over Paul George, the 2021 NBA-record twelve made threes in a single playoff game against Denver. The Bucks Year 2 bounce-back from a difficult Year 1 is real character evidence: returned from "the toughest year of my life" to a ninth All-Star selection, NBA Cup championship, and a measurable pull-up shooting jump from 32% to 42% catch-and-shoot. The career playoff resume reconciles less cleanly than the highlight reel — 25.2 PPG playoff scoring (eleventh all-time) sits against a 36.1% career playoff win percentage (tenth-lowest among players with fifty-plus playoff games) and a matched-season TS% delta of −2.9 versus regular season. The 64-game eval-window clutch line (35.7% FG, 20.7% 3PT, +1.5 plus-minus) indicates that the legacy Dame Time reputation outpaces what the recent two seasons actually showed. Defensive motor is the inverse — selective effort, documented and multi-source.

The bottom line is the injury and the age. Lillard tore his left Achilles in the first quarter of Game 4 of the 2025 first round, reaching for a long rebound, non-contact, age 34. Surgery May 2, 2025. Returns 2026-27 at age 36. The historical base rate for elite scoring point guards tearing the Achilles at 35 is severe — Chauncey Billups (Achilles February 2012, age 35) returned for 22 games at 36 in a role-player capacity and was effectively done at 37; Tony Parker (Achilles May 2017, age 35) returned the next season at 7.7 PPG off the bench and retired at 37. Both pre-injury credentials closely match Lillard's vintage. Composite reflects this directly: high Tier 6 reflects demonstrated 2024-25 perennial-All-Star form compressed by active-injury dormancy and the age-regime recovery base rate, not the prior 7x All-NBA peak. Realistic peak going forward is recovery back to 2024-25 demonstrated form; realistic floor is the Billups/Parker role-player tier within the first post-injury season.
