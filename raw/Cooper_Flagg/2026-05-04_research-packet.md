[Sub-domain #4: CAS 3PT — catch-and-shoot]

SEASON AGGREGATE VERIFICATION NOTE:
Multiple sources initially cited "39%" three-point shooting, which appears to be a conflation with Duke college stats (he shot 39% at Duke). ESPN career stats and the statistical script confirm: 72-of-244 from three (29.5%), on approximately 3.5 attempts per game over 70 games. The script shows 28.2% on 244 3PA — consistent with ESPN's 29.5% given rounding (72/244 = 29.5%). This discrepancy (28.2 vs 29.5) may reflect a small tracking difference; both figures indicate sub-30% shooting on genuine volume. The "39%" figure cited in several articles IS NOT a 2025-26 NBA season aggregate — it is a carryover of his Duke percentage or a hot-stretch figure. Flagg's three-point shooting was materially below 30% for the full 2025-26 season.

POSITIVE evidence:
- Shot 43.6% from three since December 21, 2026 — indicating genuine late-season improvement trajectory. — The Ringer, January 2026 (citing data through that date).
- "A nice high release and consistent mechanics" — NoCeilings NBA pre-draft analysis; mechanics described as the same ones "that powered his stellar shooting at Duke."
- Pull-up three-point efficiency (college-era): 40.6% on 32 attempts — NoCeilings NBA. Suggests off-dribble three-point ability exists; the problem is shot selection (forced pull-ups in poor situations) not necessarily mechanics.
- Trainer Matt MacKenzie: "When he shoots without any hesitation, things look very pure." — SI.com / multiple outlets, 2026 season.
- Coach Jason Kidd: "comfortable shooting it off the dribble, but we want him to get a little better with catch-and-shoot threes." — multiple Mavs coverage outlets, 2025-26 season. Confirms off-dribble is the relative strength.
- "He didn't have issues shooting the three in college. The mechanics are a bit unconventional, but there's no glaring flaw." — Diggin Basketball substack.
- Pre-draft historical arc: Shot 38.5% at Duke. Career pre-NBA: 33.7% on 249 attempts across HS career (Global Scouting, X, April 2024). Range of outcome is real.

NEGATIVE evidence:
- Season aggregate: approximately 28.2–29.5% from three on 244 attempts. This is the most significant offensive weakness identified by all sources.
- March 2026 specifically: 18.2% from three, went 8-of-44 from deep across 15 games — SmokingCuban.com; The Ringer.
- "Among 199 players with at least 40 three-point attempts, his mark ranked last" at one mid-season point. — search aggregate from multiple outlets.
- Ranks "182nd of 185 players attempting 100+ threes in efficiency." — The Ringer, January 2026.
- Catch-and-shoot is the weaker split: "16-for-64 — that's 25 percent — a woeful number that has to improve." — The Ringer, January 2026. Most of his attempts are coming off the dribble in creation situations rather than spot-up.
- "Issue is shot profile rather than ability with most attempts off-dribble rather than spot-up situations." — Diggin Basketball substack.
- "He hasn't had issues shooting the three, but the volume on off-dribble vs. spot-up creates an unfavorable mix." — multiple sources.
- Hesitation identified as mechanical root cause: "Hesitation is the No. 1 enemy of his progress. When there is any type of hesitation or doubt, that's when you see him miss his mark." — Matt MacKenzie (trainer), SI.com, 2026.
- Shot profile note: Only 20% of his total field goal attempts were threes (3.5 per game vs. 17.1 FGA/game) — search aggregate. Relatively low frequency for a wing; he's not forcing volume, but catch-and-shoot opportunities are limited by poor Mavericks spacing.
- "He sometimes doesn't keep his elbows tight which leads to some of his shots being offline." — NBADraft.net scouting notes.
- Pull-up mid-range: 33.3% on pull-up twos (college-era Synergy, NoCeilings NBA) — concerning for a player relying heavily on the in-between game; attributed to rushing the release.
- Offensive system context: The Mavericks ranked 28th in offense; forced Flagg to "mimic Luka Doncic's role in a dysfunctional system." — The Ringer. This amplifies the 3PT struggles by creating more off-dribble attempts in poor situations.
